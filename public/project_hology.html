Hology

empowering and informing individuals australian households to motivate sustainable energy consumption

Australia is advancing towards SDG7 (Affordable and Clean Energy) at the governmental level, yet individual energy consumption habits remain stagnant.

There's a pressing need for increased awareness, control, and motivation among people to reduce energy use. My objective was to devise a solution to address this gap.

OVERVIEW

In a collaborative university project for our graduation show, I undertook roles encompassing UX research, UX design, and product design. Our objective was to devise strategies that motivate individuals to reduce energy consumption at home. Guided by the double-diamond model, we pursued a comprehensive design process.

How can we provide Australian households with more information, control, and motivation to reduce their energy bills?

The Problem Statement

FINAL SOLUTION

We designed ‘Hology’ - a home energy meter that visually informs the household`s live energy usage and empowers and motivates people to act economically and environmentally sustainable.

DESIGN PROCESS

The project followed the double diamond model, encompassing eight iterative phases: discover, define, develop, and deliver, each addressing specific solution aspects.

Iteration 1 & 2:

Understand the problem's context, root causes, stakeholders, and needs.

Iteration 3:

Gather feedback on initial concepts.

Iteration 4:

Generate technically viable solutions meeting stakeholder needs.

Iteration 5:

Conceptualize an intuitive visual presentation of the product's information.

Iteration 6:

Finalize the product's effective communicative spatial form.

Iteration 7:

Explore interaction methods for user-friendly interfaces.

Iteration 8:

Solicit feedback to enhance the overall user experience.

Define

To define the problem, we analysed our findings to understand the context, causes, stakeholders and their needs.

Key Insights

Australia has effective sustainability and reliability strategy, but affordability improvements at the personal level are regressing. Primary stakeholders: Australian households.

The importance of offering convenience, power, and consumption control information to residential energy users.

Limited viable solutions for immediate, feasible energy cost reduction beyond unplugging appliances.

Survey results identify barriers like insufficient motivation and information, despite participants expressing a desire to cut energy expenses.

Interviews uncover core reasons behind energy cost reduction challenges:

  • 1. Users struggle to comprehend real-time energy data during consumption.
  • 2. Scarcity of convenient methods to manage energy usage effectively.
  • 3. Reduced motivation due to technical and financial limitations.
