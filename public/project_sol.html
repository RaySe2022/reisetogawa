Sol: Mindful Social Media

Redesigning addictive social media interaction to be more mindful and controllable

The remarkable expansion of social media platforms and inadequate internal design choices have fueled a pervasive issue of social media addiction among teenagers and young adults. My role was to redesign the user interaction to encourage mindful usage of social media.

OVERVIEW

As part of a university project, I assumed dual roles involving UX research and UX design. Our primary goal was to create a mobile application that addressed addiction issues associated with popular social media platforms utilizing Hook Models. We followed the systematic approach of the double-diamond model throughout our comprehensive design process.

Users often lack awareness of their own screen addiction and usage patterns. Additionally, companies tend to prioritize maximizing user engagement through the Hook Model, which results in limited user control.

The Problem Statement

FINAL SOLUTION

We created 'Sol,' an application that revolutionizes the user experience with social media by intervening in the addictive Hook Model, promoting more mindful screen usage.

Iteration 3:

Design a mid-fidelity prototype and gain insights of the app`s experience

Discover & Define

To gain insights from the user-testing, we conducted user observation, interviews and think-aloud protocol.

Key Insights

Unclear task flows - The interface was difficultto understand, due to information overload. The features of the 'customise' page was not intuitive.

Intuitive app navigation - More guidance is needed for efficient navigation. e.g. include informative headers.

Straight forward functionality - Users struggled to understand the app features due to having 'too many tools'

Develop

From the findings from our analysis, we decided to design an app that clearly communicates the functionality of the app and improve user navigation by including call-to-action buttons.

Key Insights

Lack of Intuitive Data - Most users were not sure how to interpret the analytics on the app, and felt overwhelmed by the amount of numbers it displayed.

Lack of Concept Clarity - Users also misinterpretted our app as merely on analytics app rather than a 'dashboard' app with customisable features like intended.

Lack of Empathetic Design - The excessive quantitative data lack of positive reinforcement led to a sense of guilt over usage, repelling mindful change.

Develop

From our findings, we decided to implement a strong design image, reduce numerical data, simplify the UI and incorporate more empathetic design.

Future Consideration

Our final design was able to incorporate the different elements of user psychology and data drawn from both primary and secondary research, to target specific user issues with social media addiction, offering a holistic approach towards the design problems identified through various methods and processes. By adopting a solution-driven mode of thinking through the framework of the double diamond, we were able to unveil significant insight into the true nature of consumer behaviour and ways to synthesise that data into a deliverable product, along with the iterative process of design. From the rebranding from 'Nudgeless' to our final design 'Sol', we were able discover the ways in which peconceptions relating to design often evolve in light of new data, opinions and suggestions derived from user feedback, thereby reinforcing the importance of design processes in delivering a solution that is effective to a targetted design problem. The process of lateral thinking and empathetic modelling enabled through the various idea generation exercises allowed us to adopt a creative yet controlled approach in our product development, leading to a final design that was able to holistically incorporate the strengths of each prototypes, and make necessary adjustments.

Limitations

Our final design was created with the understanding that the app was targeted to females, and therefore had a less 'gender neutral' design. This means that a large user segment will be less likely to use our app, and thus limit the coverage and thus impact of our solution on the design probtem.
