Sol: Mindful Social Media

Redesigning addictive social media interaction to be more mindful and controllable

The remarkable expansion of social media platforms and inadequate internal design choices have fueled a pervasive issue of social media addiction among teenagers and young adults. My role was to redesign the user interaction to encourage mindful usage of social media.

OVERVIEW

As part of a university project, I assumed dual roles involving UX research and UX design. Our primary goal was to create a mobile application that addressed addiction issues associated with popular social media platforms utilizing Hook Models. We followed the systematic approach of the double-diamond model throughout our comprehensive design process.

The Problem Statement

FINAL SOLUTION

We created 'Sol,' an application that revolutionizes the user experience with social media by intervening in the addictive Hook Model, promoting more mindful screen usage.

Future Consideration

Hology is currently in a minimally viable prototype state. With six more months to refine the concept, our goal is to implement additional functionalities and improve the physical properties of Hology. Our focus remains on providing users with a sense of control, information, and motivation. To improve the quality of information and its presentation, we plan to integrate IoT into Hology's design. This will allow for personalization in data type and presentation, such as selecting desired visualizations and more personalized data. In addition, an "information" mode will be available on other devices. Studies have shown that providing vivid personal information can motivate cost-reducing behavior. Our predicted design direction of implementing IoT will enable us to better respond to our goal. Regarding the physical properties of Hology, we will focus on the shape of the enclosure. Our research shows a preference for a wider angle of visibility for the hologram. Additionally, brightness of the hologram was a recurring concern raised during testing. To address these issues, we plan to create a prism-shaped screen using dark-colored, transparent materials. This will allow the hologram to be visible from all sides and provide a more refined display, similar to the product 'Holo' shown in the image below.
